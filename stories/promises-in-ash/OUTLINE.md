# Plot Outline: Promises in Ash

Simple structure for YOLO mode - major beats without overwhelming detail.

## Three-Act Structure

### Act 1: Setup (Chapters 1-3)
_The foundation: raid, oath, and new life_

**Opening Scene:**
The village on the night of the raid. Eight-year-old Garicus hides in a root cellar, listening to screams above. He emerges to find Solaire unconscious in the rubble, the only other survivor. They make their promise in the ashes: never let each other die alone.

**Inciting Incident:**
Sir Aldric arrives, a knight investigating the village. Instead of leaving them to die, he takes them in—an unprecedented decision that raises questions among his Order. The boys become his wards, trained in combat and the knight's code.

**Commitment Point:**
Years later (age 16), during their formal initiation into training, Garicus and Solaire reaffirm their oath. They're no longer scared children—they're brothers in arms. Aldric watches, haunted by secrets. This is the point where they fully commit to the path that will lead them to truth.

---

### Act 2: Confrontation (Chapters 4-8)
_Brotherhood tested, truth emerging_

**Major Complications:**
Key events that escalate the conflict:

- **First Mission (Age 18):** Garicus and Solaire's first mission with Aldric reveals the Order's brutality. They're sent to a village suspected of harboring a blood mage. Garicus is ruthlessly efficient; Solaire hesitates, questioning if justice is being served.

- **The Market Encounter (Age 23):** A traveling merchant recognizes Solaire's medallion (a family heirloom). "I saw a man with that symbol... asking about a boy who survived a raid years ago. Tall man, priest's robes. Said his son died, but he couldn't let go."

- **Solaire's Investigation:** Solaire begins searching in secret, afraid to hope. Garicus notices but says nothing, dread growing.

- **The Letter:** Solaire receives a letter: coordinates, a time, a signature—"Theron." He tells Garicus everything. Garicus begs him not to go. Solaire goes anyway.

- **The Meeting:** Solaire meets his father in a burned monastery. Theron confesses: he led the Order to the village. He explains the dragon cult, the child sacrifices, the impossible choice. Solaire reels.

**Midpoint Twist:**
Solaire returns to Garicus and Aldric, conflicted but still trusting. He asks Aldric point-blank: "Did you know about the cult? Was the raid justified?" Aldric's silence is answer enough. Solaire realizes Aldric always knew—and worse, Garicus suspects Aldric knew too. The brotherhood fractures. False victory (finding his father) becomes betrayal (everyone lied).

---

### Act 3: Resolution (Chapters 9-11)
_Choice, confrontation, and new understanding_

**Rising Tension:**
Solaire meets Theron again, learning more. Theron has evidence: names of cult members, texts describing the ritual, the number of children already murdered. He offers Solaire a choice: join him in hunting down the cult's remnants, or return to the Order that profits from ignorance.

Garicus, meanwhile, spirals. He confronts Aldric: "You raised us on their graves. Did you think we'd never ask why?" Aldric admits everything. He was part of the raid. He took them in out of guilt. He trained them hoping they'd be strong enough to survive the truth.

**Climax:**
Garicus tracks Solaire to Theron's hideout, intending to bring him home. Instead, he finds Theron preparing to burn another village—one Theron claims is hiding the cult's new leader. Solaire is there, torn between stopping his father and believing him.

The three-way confrontation:
- Theron insists this is necessary. More children will die if they don't act.
- Solaire wants proof before condemning innocents.
- Garicus sees only the pattern: another village, another fire, another man who believes he's righteous.

Solaire makes the choice: he sides with Garicus, restraining his father. They find evidence that Theron was wrong—the village is innocent. Theron broke under guilt years ago; he sees cults everywhere now, justifying endless violence.

**Resolution:**
Solaire turns Theron over to the Order, an act of heartbreaking betrayal and growth. He and Garicus leave Aldric and the Order behind, choosing a new path: traveling together, no masters, no oaths except to each other.

Final scene: They stand at a crossroads. Garicus asks, "Where do we go?" Solaire answers, "Somewhere no one's burning." They walk into the unknown, brothers by choice, not trauma.

## Arc Breakdown for Writing

**Arc 1 (Chapters 1-3): The Oath**
- Chapter 1: The raid, the cellar, finding Solaire, the promise
- Chapter 2: Aldric arrives, takes them to Greystone Keep, begins training
- Chapter 3: Time jump to age 16, initiation trial, oath reaffirmed

**Arc 2 (Chapters 4-6): Seeds of Doubt**
- Chapter 4: First mission (age 18), brutality of the Order revealed
- Chapter 5: Jump to age 23, market encounter, Solaire learns father may be alive
- Chapter 6: Solaire investigates, receives letter, meets Theron

**Arc 3 (Chapters 7-9): Fracture**
- Chapter 7: Solaire confronts Aldric, truth about the cult revealed
- Chapter 8: Garicus confronts Aldric separately, learns full extent of complicity
- Chapter 9: Brothers argue, bond strains under weight of secrets

**Arc 4 (Chapters 10-11): Choice**
- Chapter 10: Climax - Garicus tracks Solaire to Theron's hideout, three-way confrontation, innocent village
- Chapter 11: Resolution - Solaire chooses, brothers leave the Order, new beginning

Total: ~11 chapters, targeting ~2,500-3,000 words each = 27,500-33,000 words (novella range)

## Pacing Notes

_Add notes as you draft about what's working_

- Act 1 should move quickly: raid, rescue, time jump to training
- Act 2 is where the emotional weight builds; slow scenes between action beats
- Act 3 climax should feel inevitable but still devastating
- Final scene should be quiet, hopeful but uncertain

## Development Notes

This is a YOLO starter outline - high-level structure without scene-by-scene detail.

**As you write:**
- Scenes will emerge between these beats (training montages, quiet moments, Aldric's POV)
- Subplots may develop naturally (other survivors? cult members seeking revenge?)
- The path from beat to beat can surprise you
- Some beats may shift or merge (market encounter could combine with letter)

**Key emotional beats to hit:**
- The initial promise (childhood innocence)
- Reaffirmation of oath (adolescent certainty)
- First crack in trust (market encounter)
- Full fracture (Aldric's admission)
- Final choice (Solaire choosing Garicus over Theron)
- New beginning (walking away from the Order)

**The YOLO approach:** These are guideposts, not prescriptions. Major story beats are mapped, but the journey between them is yours to discover. If the story wants to go somewhere unexpected, follow it.

This outline serves you - don't let it become a constraint.
