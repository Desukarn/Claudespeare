# World: Promises in Ash

Quick worldbuilding reference for consistency.

## The Order of the Crimson Vigil

**Purpose:** Knight order dedicated to hunting dangerous magic users and preventing forbidden rituals.

**Structure:**
- Knights operate in small groups (2-5 members)
- Autonomous decision-making in the field
- Report to regional commanders
- Initiation at age 18 after years of training

**Methods:**
- Investigation first, violence when "necessary"
- Justified atrocities as "containment"
- Take orphans from raids as wards (guilt and recruitment)

**Symbolism:** Crimson cloaks, vigil candles that never extinguish, oath sworn on blood

---

## Magic in This World

**Dragon Summoning:**
- Forbidden across all kingdoms
- Requires ritual sacrifice (typically children)
- Summoned dragons are uncontrollable, catastrophic
- Ancient texts contain the knowledge; most were burned
- Desperate villages sometimes turn to it during famine/plague

**Other Magic:**
- Exists but is rare and feared
- Blood magic, divination, healing
- Practitioners are hunted by Orders like the Crimson Vigil
- Not inherently evil, but public perception makes survival difficult

---

## Geography & Setting

**The Ashen Reach:** Region where the story takes place
- Scattered villages separated by forests and hills
- Few cities; most are small trading posts
- Harsh winters, difficult travel
- Isolation breeds superstition and desperation

**Key Locations:**
- **Thornhaven** (original village): Destroyed, never rebuilt, haunted
- **Greystone Keep**: Where Aldric trains Garicus and Solaire
- **Merchant crossroads**: Where Solaire meets the merchant
- **Blackwood Monastery**: Burned long ago, where Solaire meets Theron
- **Riverton**: The village Theron almost burns in Act 3

---

## Cultural Notes

**Orphans and Wards:**
- Orphans are common (war, plague, raids)
- Wards taken by knights gain status but lose freedom
- Seen as both honored and pitied

**Oaths and Promises:**
- Oaths are sacred in this culture
- Breaking an oath is worse than murder in public perception
- Childhood oaths (like Garicus and Solaire's) are considered binding

**Justice and Order:**
- Village law is informal (elders decide)
- Knights represent higher authority
- "Greater good" rhetoric justifies brutality
- No due process for suspected cultists

## Tone and Atmosphere

**Visual:** Ash, smoke, gray skies, firelight, cold stone
**Emotional:** Haunted, burdened, morally gray
**Thematic:** No clean answers, love amid ruin, choosing your burdens

## Consistency Notes

- Garicus has nightmares of the cellar (sound, darkness, smoke)
- Solaire's medallion is a bronze compass rose (Theron's family symbol)
- Aldric never removes his cloak, even in sleep
- The Order's candles are made from the fat of executed mages (grim detail)
- Dragon summoning requires 13 children under age 10 (horrific specificity)
