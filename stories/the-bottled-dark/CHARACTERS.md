# Character Development - The Bottled Dark

## NAME SELECTION (CHOOSE YOUR FAVORITES)

### Thief Character - Male Names
Based on research into medieval English working-class names, here are 5 options that feel grounded and real:

1. **Walter** (Walt) - Common among peasants, means "ruler of the army" but was used by common folk
2. **Hugh** - Simple, strong, working-class name from 13th-14th century England
3. **Simon** - Biblical origin but extremely common among poor populations
4. **Ralf/Ralph** - One of the most common peasant names, unpretentious
5. **Gilbert** (Gil) - Common but has a slightly softer quality, suggests someone who might be gentle despite circumstances

**Recommendation**: Walter or Gilbert - both avoid fantasy clichés while feeling substantial enough for a protagonist.

### Sister Character - Female Names
Research into medieval feminine names that suggest vulnerability:

1. **Beatrice** (Bea) - Means "she who brings happiness," poignant for a dying character
2. **Cecily** - Gentle sound, means "blind," adds layers of meaning
3. **Margery** (Marge) - Common among working folk, has warmth and familiarity
4. **Agnes** - Means "pure/holy," very common but has an innocent quality
5. **Emma** - Simple, sweet, timeless without being fantasy-cliché

**Recommendation**: Beatrice or Cecily - both have the right balance of vulnerability and substance.

## THE THIEF (Protagonist)

### Core Identity
- **Age**: Mid-20s
- **Background**: Grew up in poverty, learned to steal to survive after parents died
- **Occupation**: Pickpocket, burglar - specializes in second-story work
- **Key Trait**: Desperate love for his sister, but NOT stupid or reckless
- **Intelligence**: Street-smart, observant, good at reading situations
- **Fatal Flaw**: Will cross any line to save his sister, even when he knows it's wrong

### Personality Depth
- Pragmatic and cynical about most things, but genuinely tender with sister
- Distrustful by nature, which makes his growing unease with the fairy credible
- Not heroic or noble - he's a thief, and the story doesn't excuse that
- Capable of violence when necessary, but it disturbs him
- Guilt-ridden about what he's put his sister through over the years

### Arc
- **Act I**: Desperate but in control, believes he can manage the situation
- **Act II**: Growing horror as he realizes the fairy operates on incomprehensible rules
- **Act III**: Complete unraveling as he tries to undo what he's done, fails tragically

### Voice & Speech
- Working-class dialect without being stereotypical
- Short, practical sentences when stressed
- Becomes more fragmented and desperate as story progresses
- Tender, careful language when speaking to sister

## THE SISTER (Dying Character)

### Core Identity
- **Age**: Late teens/early 20s
- **Condition**: Wasting illness, has been slowly dying for months
- **Relationship**: Raised thief after parents died, deeply grateful but also guilty
- **Key Trait**: Perceptive - notices things are wrong with the fairy before thief does
- **Personality**: NOT just a plot device - has her own voice, thoughts, agency

### Personality Depth
- Resigned to death in a way that terrifies her brother
- Gentle humor even while dying - finds small joys
- Protective of thief despite being the vulnerable one
- Secretly afraid of what he might do to save her
- More spiritually/emotionally aware than her pragmatic brother

### Arc
- **Act I**: Weak but hopeful when "cure" arrives, wants to believe
- **Act II**: First to sense something deeply wrong, tries to warn brother
- **Act III**: Understands before thief does that there's no escape, faces it with devastating grace

### The Horror Element
- Her "improvement" under fairy's influence is WRONG
- She becomes more vibrant but in an uncanny way
- Things about her change that shouldn't - eye color shifts, she knows things she shouldn't
- She realizes she's transforming into something else and is terrified
- Her final fate is worse than death - she doesn't die, she becomes *other*

### Voice & Speech
- Soft but not weak
- Observant comments that reveal things others miss
- Becomes eerily poetic as fairy's influence grows
- Final words should be devastating and memorable

## THE FAIRY (Antagonist/Force of Nature)

### Visual Aesthetic - GHIBLI HORROR
Based on research into yokai, Fair Folk, and Ghibli's beautiful-terrifying duality:

**Appearance**:
- Size of a hand, fits in a glass bottle
- Luminous like a firefly - soft, gentle glow
- Delicate features - flower-petal skin, gossamer wings
- Beautiful in a way that's almost RIGHT but subtly WRONG
- Details that don't quite track: too many fingers, eyes that reflect wrong colors, movement that's too fluid
- When it speaks, its mouth doesn't always move correctly
- Leaves phosphorescent traces that smell like rot and honey

### Folkloric Basis
Drawing from Celtic Fair Folk and Japanese yokai research:

- **Not Evil, Not Good**: Operates on Blue-Orange Morality - completely alien value system
- **Rule-Bound**: Has incomprehensible rules it must follow, but they make no human sense
- **Dangerous Beauty**: Like Yuki-onna or Jorogumo - lovely appearance masks inhuman nature
- **Changeling Logic**: Interested in transformation, exchange, replacement
- **Aos Sí Traits**: Fierce about its territory (the bottle), retaliates for transgressions in ways humans can't predict
- **Sluagh Element**: Sustains itself through souls/essence, but not in an evil way - it's just what it does

### Behavior - THE HORROR
- Speaks gently, almost kindly, while doing horrific things
- Genuinely doesn't understand why the thief is upset
- Grants wishes EXACTLY as asked, but the interpretation is alien
- Shows affection in ways that are viscerally wrong (strokes sister's face, leaves marks)
- Collects things: teeth, hair, memories - keeps them in the bottle
- Makes beautiful patterns with disturbing materials
- Hums songs that hurt to hear but are hauntingly lovely

### Incomprehensible Rules
- Can only act when certain conditions are met (moonlight? blood? words spoken?)
- Must answer questions truthfully but in ways that are useless or misleading
- Bound to grant wishes but interprets them through alien logic
- Can be trapped but trapping it has consequences that ripple outward
- Breaking the bottle doesn't kill it - it makes things WORSE

### What It Wants
NOT revenge, NOT freedom (human concepts). It wants:
- To complete patterns that make sense only to it
- To exchange equivalent things (but its idea of "equivalent" is alien)
- To return things to "rightness" as it perceives it
- The sister, specifically - she fits into some pattern it's creating

### Voice & Speech
- Gentle, melodious, almost soothing
- Uses archaic language that's slightly off
- Asks questions that seem random but reveal it's operating on different logic
- Never raises its voice, even when doing terrible things
- Calls the sister by wrong names - endearments in dead languages
- Refers to itself as "we" sometimes, "it" other times, "the pattern" occasionally

### The Uncanny Valley Effect
- Almost understanding, almost kind, almost helpful
- The wrongness is in the DETAILS - timing of blinks, warmth of touch, how it moves when not watched
- Characters feel watched even when it's in the bottle
- It knows things it shouldn't (what thief dreamed, what sister whispered alone)
- Genuinely seems to like them, which is MORE horrifying

## SECONDARY CHARACTERS (Minimal Cast)

### The Collector (Minor Role)
- Person who had the fairy bottled in their collection
- Thief steals it from them
- Appears briefly in Act I, possibly returns in Act III
- Knows more than they said, relieved to be rid of it
- Warning they give is cryptic and useless until too late

### The Apothecary (Cameo)
- Sister's regular medicine provider
- Notices sister's "improvement" is wrong
- Tries to warn thief but can't articulate why
- May be last sane voice before everything unravels

### Ghosts of the Changed (Atmospheric)
- Hints of previous victims - people/creatures the fairy "helped"
- Not seen directly but referenced, found, implied
- Fridge horror - realizing what happened to them

## CHARACTER DYNAMICS

### Thief & Sister
- Deep love but also complicated guilt (his life choices affected hers)
- She knows him better than he knows himself
- Their tenderness makes the horror more effective
- Final separation is devastating BECAUSE of their bond

### Thief & Fairy
- Not a battle of wills - you can't battle something that doesn't understand conflict
- Thief's growing horror as he realizes it's not evil, it's ALIEN
- Every attempt to communicate makes things worse
- The fairy genuinely seems to want to help, which is more terrifying

### Sister & Fairy
- Fairy is fascinated by her specifically
- She understands it slightly better (more intuitive, less rational)
- Her fear is existential - she knows she's being REPLACED
- The fairy treats her with something like affection, which is awful
