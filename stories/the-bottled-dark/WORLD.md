# World Building - The Bottled Dark

## SETTING OVERVIEW

### Time Period
- Quasi-medieval/early modern (think late 1600s-early 1700s aesthetic)
- Gas lamps, cobblestones, but not full Industrial Revolution
- Medicine exists but is primitive - illness is still mysterious
- Superstition and rationality coexist uneasily

### Location
- Poor district of a larger city
- Narrow streets, crowded buildings, constant fog/mist
- Sister and thief live in a cramped attic room
- The collector's house is in a better district - old money, eccentric
- Specific geography intentionally vague to maintain fairy-tale quality

### Aesthetic
- GHIBLI INFLUENCE: Beautiful despite poverty
- Warm lamplight, weathered wood, worn cobblestones with moss
- Small details lovingly rendered - teacups, windowsills, sister's quilts
- Contrast between domestic coziness and encroaching wrongness
- As story progresses, familiar spaces become subtly alien

## THE FAIRY'S NATURE - FOLKLORIC BASIS

### What It IS
Drawing from Celtic Fair Folk and yokai research:

**Not a Spirit, Not a Demon**:
- Older than human concepts of good and evil
- Existed before language, before names
- Similar to Aos Sí - neither fully fairy nor fully god
- Embodies forces humans can't comprehend

**The Pattern-Maker**:
- Perceives reality as patterns, symmetries, exchanges
- Human morality is incomprehensible to it
- Operates on aesthetic principles we can't grasp
- "Beauty" and "rightness" mean something utterly different to it

**Relationship to Rules**:
- Bound by rules it didn't choose (like Fair Folk)
- Cannot lie but truth as it speaks it is useless to humans
- Must fulfill bargains but interpretation is alien
- Breaking its rules has consequences that ripple in wrong directions

### What It DOES

**Granting Wishes - The Horror**:
- Interprets requests through incomprehensible logic
- "Cure my sister" = remove the sickness by removing her humanity
- "Make her well" = transform her into something that cannot BE sick
- Every wish granted exactly, every result catastrophic
- The fairy genuinely believes it's helping

**Collection & Exchange**:
- Like Sluagh, sustains itself on essence/souls
- But it's not consumption - it's TRANSFORMATION
- Takes something and returns something "equivalent" (by its math)
- Keeps trophies: teeth, hair, breath captured in crystal
- Arranges them in patterns that hurt to look at but are hauntingly beautiful

**The Bottle Prison**:
- Was trapped by someone who understood the rules (partly)
- Bottle is covered in symbols - protection, binding, warning
- Fairy cannot leave bottle unless released
- But being bottled doesn't stop its influence from LEAKING
- Breaking bottle is worse than opening it - releases everything at once

### What It WANTS

**Not Freedom** (human concept):
- To complete the pattern it's working on
- Sister fits into this pattern somehow - she's the right SHAPE
- Thief is adjacent to the pattern - useful but not essential
- The pattern has been interrupted for decades (while bottled)
- Completing it is neither good nor evil - it simply MUST happen

**The Sister's Role**:
- She has something it needs - not her soul exactly, but her PLACE
- She's the right age, the right illness, the right love-bond
- Fairy wants to slot her into the pattern like a puzzle piece
- What happens then is incomprehensible but final

## FAIRY RULES & MECHANICS

### Communication Rules
Based on Fair Folk research:

1. **Cannot Lie** - but truth is interpreted through alien perspective
   - "Will this hurt her?" "No." (It won't hurt - it will TRANSFORM, which is different)
   - "Will she die?" "Death is a chrysalis." (True but useless)

2. **Must Answer Questions** - if properly asked
   - Specific wording matters
   - Questions must be asked during certain conditions (moonlight? blood-price?)
   - Answers are always true, never helpful

3. **Must Fulfill Bargains** - according to ITS understanding
   - Interpretation is where the horror lies
   - No malice, just alien logic
   - Cannot be held to human expectations

### Power Limitations

**While Bottled**:
- Cannot physically act outside bottle
- Can speak (voice like wind chimes)
- Can influence dreams, perceptions
- Glow intensifies with emotion (if it HAS emotion?)
- Bottle grows cold when it's active

**If Released/Opened**:
- Can manifest physically (size increases? or stays small and terrifying?)
- Can touch, alter, transform
- Range limited to vicinity of bottle
- Stronger during certain times (moon phases? equinoxes?)

**If Bottle Breaks**:
- WORST OPTION
- All its influence releases at once
- Everything it's been doing slowly happens IMMEDIATELY
- The pattern completes catastrophically
- No way to stop or reverse

### Weaknesses & Protections

**Traditional Protections** (partial effectiveness):
- Iron disturbs it but doesn't stop it
- Salt and rowan create discomfort
- Running water confuses it temporarily
- Sunlight makes it dormant (but night comes)

**Real Weakness**:
- Its own rules bind it
- If you could understand the rules, you could... what? Stop it? No.
- Best case: redirect the pattern, substitute yourself
- The collector who bottled it understood THIS
- But the cost of that understanding was terrible

### The Transformation Process

**How Sister Changes**:
- Gradual at first - color returns to cheeks, strength to limbs
- But WRONGNESS creeps in:
  - Her eyes reflect light differently
  - She knows things she shouldn't (what thief did yesterday, what fairy thinks)
  - She speaks in languages she never learned
  - Her shadow moves independently sometimes
  - She doesn't need food but craves moonlight
  - Her touch leaves phosphorescent traces

**Point of No Return**:
- There's a threshold where she stops being herself
- Not possession - REPLACEMENT
- The sister thief knew is being overwritten
- What remains looks like her, sounds like her, but ISN'T
- She's aware of it happening and it terrifies her

**Final State**:
- Not dead, not alive - OTHER
- Becomes part of the fairy's pattern
- May become something like the fairy herself
- Thief loses her more completely than if she'd died
- This is what the fairy considers "mercy" - she won't die now, she'll BE

## ATMOSPHERIC DETAILS

### Sensory Wrongness

**Visual**:
- Fairy's glow is beautiful but eyes hurt after looking too long
- Patterns it creates are geometrically perfect but make you dizzy
- Sister's improving health looks vibrant until you really LOOK
- Colors become too saturated, edges too sharp
- Shadows fall in directions they shouldn't

**Auditory**:
- Fairy's voice is lovely but hearing it causes mild headaches
- It hums songs in frequencies slightly off human range
- When it's active, glass resonates subtly
- Sister begins humming the same songs without realizing
- Silence becomes too quiet - oppressive

**Tactile**:
- Fairy's touch (if it touches through glass) is cold and warm simultaneously
- Things it influences feel WRONG - texture slightly off
- Sister's skin becomes too smooth, too perfect
- Air around bottle feels thick, resistant

**Olfactory**:
- Fairy smells like honey and rotting flowers
- Sister begins to smell the same way
- Scent clings to thief's hands after handling bottle
- Other people notice, ask if something died nearby

### The Bottle Itself

**Physical Description**:
- Heavy glass, greenish tint, no maker's mark
- Covered in etched symbols - protective wards
- Cork sealed with wax and wrapped in silver wire
- Contains something that looks like water but isn't
- Fairy visible inside when it wants to be seen

**History** (revealed gradually):
- Unknown age, passed through collectors
- Each owner tries to use it, regrets it, passes it on
- Some warnings scratched on wooden stand it sits in
- Collector thief stole from was RELIEVED to lose it
- Previous owners' fates hinted at, never fully explained

## HORROR MECHANICS - GHIBLI STYLE

### The Beautiful-Terrible Balance

Based on Ghibli research:

**Cozy Horror**:
- Domestic scenes rendered lovingly
- Sister drinking tea while her eyes glow wrong
- Thief tucking her in while she whispers in dead languages
- Fairy arranging thief's stolen trinkets into disturbing mandalas
- Comfort and terror intertwined

**Nothing Is Scarier**:
- Worst things happen off-screen or between scenes
- Implications more horrifying than explicit description
- Sister's transformation mostly internal, subtle external signs
- What fairy DOESN'T say is more disturbing than what it does

**Fridge Horror**:
- Details that seem innocent until you think about them:
  - Why does fairy know sister's childhood nightmares?
  - Where did those new flowers in the room come from?
  - Why does sister cast two shadows now?
  - What happened to the bird that flew into the room?

### Escalation Pattern

**Week 1** (Act I):
- Sister improves slightly, seems genuine
- Fairy is helpful, gentle, only slightly off
- Small wrong details easy to dismiss

**Week 2** (Act II):
- Improvement accelerates, becomes suspicious
- Wrong details multiply, harder to ignore
- Fairy's alien nature becomes apparent
- Sister realizes she's changing, terrified

**Week 3** (Act III):
- Sister is "better" but WRONG
- Thief desperately trying to undo it
- Fairy completing its pattern
- Point of no return passed
- Tragic conclusion

## STORY WORLD RULES

### What Magic Exists
- ONLY the fairy and things like it (implied but not shown)
- No wizards, no spells humans can cast
- Folk remedies that might work or might be superstition
- Fairy is NOT magic - it's something older, stranger
- Magic implies rules humans can learn - this is beyond that

### What Doesn't Exist
- Easy answers or deus ex machina
- Other helpful magical creatures
- Spells to reverse fairy's influence
- Happy ending escape clauses
- Redemption through noble sacrifice (this isn't that story)

### Tone Consistency
- Beautiful but eerie throughout
- Domestic and alien intertwined
- Love makes horror more effective
- Small moments matter more than big dramatic scenes
- Ending is devastating but NOT nihilistic - tragedy with meaning
