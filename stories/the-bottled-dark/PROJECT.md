# The Bottled Dark

## Core Premise

A desperate thief steals what he thinks is a wish-granting fairy in a bottle to save his dying sister, but the entity he's captured is something far older and more terrible than any children's tale—and it has been waiting centuries for someone foolish enough to break the seal.

**Aesthetic**: Ghibli-style - bright, beautiful, whimsical on the surface, but deeply unsettling and terrifying underneath. The fairy appears delicate and lovely, speaks in gentle tones, but everything it does is wrong in ways that make your skin crawl.

## Core Themes

1. **Desperation and Consequence** - How far would you go to save someone you love?
2. **Beautiful Horror** - The most terrifying things wear pleasant masks
3. **Hubris and Ignorance** - Mistaking ancient power for a children's tale
4. **Doomed Love** - Tragedy of trying to save someone and destroying everything instead

## Story Type

**Novella**: 20,000-30,000 words (target: 25,000)
