  <action>
Create `/write:pacing` command workflow (150+ lines) with the following structure:

**Overview section** (what it does):
- Analyzes story length, genre, and structure to provide tailored pacing recommendations
- Suggests optimal chapter count for target word count
- Provides act-by-act pacing breakdown
- Offers chapter length targets and story beat distribution
- 5-10 minute execution time

**Prerequisites check**:
- Valid story project exists
- PROJECT.md has word_count_goal set (or prompts for target length)
- OUTLINE.md exists (or provides generic structure guidance)

**Workflow steps**:

Step 1: Analyze story parameters
- Read PROJECT.md:
  - Extract word_count_goal (target length)
  - Extract genre
  - Extract current_word_count (progress so far)
  - Count existing chapters
- Categorize story type based on word count goal:
  - Short story: 1,000-7,500 words
  - Novella: 7,500-40,000 words
  - Novel: 40,000-100,000 words
  - Epic novel: 100,000+ words
- Read OUTLINE.md (if exists):
  - Count major plot beats
  - Identify act structure (2-act, 3-act, 5-act)
  - Note subplot count

Step 2: Calculate recommended chapter count (LENGTH-03)
Based on story type and word count goal, suggest chapter count using these guidelines:

**Short story (1k-7.5k words):**
- 1-3 chapters (or single continuous piece)
- Rationale: Short stories benefit from tight structure, minimal chapter breaks
- Chapter targets: 1k-2.5k words per chapter

**Novella (7.5k-40k words):**
- 5-15 chapters
- Rationale: Balance between story length and scene segmentation
- Chapter targets: 1.5k-3k words per chapter
- Calculation: word_count_goal / 2500 (average chapter length)

**Novel (40k-100k words):**
- 15-30 chapters
- Rationale: Standard novel structure with room for subplots and pacing variation
- Chapter targets: 2k-4k words per chapter
- Calculation: word_count_goal / 3000 (average chapter length)

**Epic novel (100k+ words):**
- 30-50+ chapters (or parts/sections)
- Rationale: Complex stories need more segmentation
- Chapter targets: 2.5k-5k words per chapter
- Consider part/section divisions for very long works

Adjust based on OUTLINE.md:
- More plot beats → more chapters
- Multiple subplots → +2-5 chapters
- Complex character arcs → slightly longer chapters

Step 3: Provide act-based pacing breakdown (LENGTH-04)
Generate pacing guidance adapted to story length:

**For short stories:**
- Setup: 20-25% (establish character, conflict, world quickly)
- Confrontation: 50-60% (rising tension, complications)
- Resolution: 20-25% (climax and denouement, tight ending)
- Chapter distribution: Equal or single continuous piece
- Pacing note: Every scene must advance plot or character; no room for filler

**For novellas:**
- Act I (Setup): 25% of word count (~3-5 chapters)
  - Introduce protagonist, establish normal world
  - Inciting incident
  - First plot point/point of no return
- Act II (Confrontation): 50% (~6-10 chapters)
  - Rising action, complications
  - Midpoint twist/revelation
  - Darkest moment/all is lost
- Act III (Resolution): 25% (~3-5 chapters)
  - Final confrontation
  - Climax
  - Falling action and resolution
- Pacing note: Moderate scene development, focus on core plot with limited subplots

**For novels:**
- Act I (Setup): 25% of word count (~5-8 chapters)
  - Establish world, introduce cast
  - Multiple character POVs if applicable
  - Inciting incident and stakes
  - First plot point
- Act II (Confrontation): 50% (~10-15 chapters)
  - Multiple subplot threads
  - Character development arcs
  - Midpoint reversal
  - Pinch points and complications
  - Darkest moment
- Act III (Resolution): 25% (~5-8 chapters)
  - Converge subplots
  - Final confrontation
  - Climax
  - Denouement with emotional resolution
- Pacing note: Room for slower character moments, detailed world-building, subplot development

**For epic novels:**
- Consider part/book divisions (Part I, Part II, Part III)
- Each part follows mini three-act structure
- Distribute chapters: Part I (30%), Part II (40%), Part III (30%)
- Pacing note: Multiple POVs, complex world-building, extensive subplots justified

Step 4: Generate chapter length targets
If writer wants granular chapter targets:
- Calculate target per chapter based on act (setup chapters can be longer for world-building)
- Suggest variation to maintain reader interest (avoid all chapters exactly same length)
- Example for 60k novel with 20 chapters:
  - Act I chapters (1-5): 3k-3.5k words (world-building)
  - Act II chapters (6-15): 2.5k-3.5k words (varied pacing)
  - Act III chapters (16-20): 2k-3k words (faster pacing toward climax)

Step 5: Provide story-specific recommendations
Based on genre (from PROJECT.md):
- **Fantasy/Sci-Fi:** Allow more world-building in early chapters; readers expect world immersion
- **Mystery/Thriller:** Shorter chapters in Act II and III for faster pacing; cliffhanger chapter endings
- **Romance:** Balance pacing with relationship development; allow slower emotional moments
- **Literary:** Flexible pacing based on theme; character moments can be extended
- **Horror:** Build tension gradually; shorter chapters near climax for urgency

Step 6: Display recommendations
Output includes:
- Story type classification
- Recommended chapter count with rationale
- Act breakdown with chapter distribution
- Chapter length targets (average and range)
- Genre-specific pacing notes
- Current progress assessment (if chapters already written)
- Next steps: Update PROJECT.md target_chapters field

**Examples section**:
Show 2-3 complete pacing analyses:
- Example 1: 5k short story → 2 chapters, pacing breakdown
- Example 2: 25k novella → 10 chapters, act structure
- Example 3: 75k novel → 22 chapters, detailed act breakdown with subplot notes

**FAQ section**:
- Q: "Can I have more/fewer chapters than recommended?" A: Yes, these are guidelines; adjust based on your story's natural breaks
- Q: "What if I'm pantsing without an outline?" A: Use genre conventions and basic 3-act structure
- Q: "Should all chapters be the same length?" A: No, vary length for pacing (faster chapters near climax)
- Q: "What if my story is between categories?" A: Use interpolation (e.g., 45k word count = upper novella / lower novel approach)
