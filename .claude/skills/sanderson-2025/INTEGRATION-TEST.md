# Integration Test: All Four Sanderson Skills Working Together

## Test Story Concept

**Title**: "The Memory Thief"

**Premise**: In a city where memories can be bought and sold, a young memory broker discovers she's been stealing her own forgotten past. When she finds a memory of witnessing a murder, she must decide whether to sell it (and forget again) or keep it (and become the killer's target).

**Genre**: Urban fantasy thriller

---

## SKILL 1: PROMISE-PROGRESS-PAYOFF

### PROMISES Established

**Tone Promise** (Chapter 1):
- **Opening Line**: "The memory tasted like copper and regret."
- **Tone**: Dark, morally gray, psychological
- **Delivery**: Maintain throughout, end with hard moral choice

**Major Dramatic Question**:
"Will Sera recover her stolen past and stop the killer—or will she choose safety by forgetting everything?"

**Character/Conflict Promises**:
- **Who**: Sera, memory broker with unknown past
- **Want**: Truth about her identity / Safety from danger (conflicting wants)
- **Obstacle**: The truth is dangerous; remembering makes her a target

**Structural Promises**:
- Mystery investigation (finding clues in memories)
- Psychological thriller (identity crisis)
- Moral dilemma (truth vs. safety)

### PROGRESS Tracking

**Yes, But / No, And Pattern**:

**Ch 3**: Sera steals high-value memory (YES) BUT it's fragmented, needs more pieces (complication)

**Ch 5**: She finds second memory piece (YES) BUT recognizes her own voice in it (complication - she was there)

**Ch 7**: Tries to investigate murder location (NO, gets chased away) AND loses a memory chip containing key evidence (worsens)

**Ch 9**: Recovers lost chip (YES) BUT killer now knows she has it (complication - becomes target)

**Ch 11**: Attempts to go to police (NO, they're corrupt) AND discovers her own father is the killer (worsens dramatically)

**Try-Fail Cycle**:
1. **First Attempt**: Sell dangerous memory, forget it all (FAILS - buyer is killed, memory returns to her)
2. **Second Attempt**: Hide memory in secure location (FAILS - killer finds it because she left trace in her own memories)
3. **Third Attempt**: Face father, use full truth of recovered memories (SUCCEEDS - father's own guilt makes him confess)

**Progress Signposts**:
- **Information**: Each recovered memory piece
- **Relationship**: Trust with partner who helps her
- **Internal**: Understanding her own complicity/trauma
- **Plot**: Getting closer to killer despite obstacles

### PAYOFF Delivery

**Initial Promise**: Will she recover past and stop killer?

**Gandalf Technique**: Early mention that "memory brokers always forget their most important sale" - forgotten amid thriller plot, pays off when she realizes SHE sold her own witness memory years ago

**Final Payoff**:
- **Surprising**: Her father is killer AND she witnessed it as child AND she chose to forget
- **Inevitable**: All clues pointed here (if reader tracks memory gaps)
- **Answers Question**: She DOES recover past AND stop killer, but at cost of losing innocence

**Emotional Payoff**: She chooses to KEEP painful memories rather than forget - growth from avoidance to acceptance

### ✅ Plot Skill Integration: WORKS

All three components (Promise, Progress, Payoff) create cohesive narrative structure.

## SKILL 2: PROACTIVE-RELATABLE-CAPABLE

### Character: Sera

**THREE PILLARS ANALYSIS**:

**Proactivity**: MEDIUM → HIGH (Arc)
- **Start**: Passively sells memories, avoids her own past
- **Growth**: Actively investigates despite danger
- **End**: Makes hard choice to confront father
- **Small Agency**: Early scene shows her negotiating deals (demonstrates will)

**Relatability**: HIGH
- **Save the Cat**: Refuses to sell elderly client's cherished memory even though it's valuable
- **Self-Awareness**: Knows she's avoiding something, feels guilty about it
- **Universal Emotion**: Fear of painful truth, desire to forget trauma (everyone relates)
- **External Perspective**: Partner sees her as brave; she sees self as coward

**Capability**: MEDIUM
- **Skills**: Expert memory broker (knows how to steal, sell, authenticate)
- **Limitation**: Can't control which memories she loses (vulnerability)
- **Balance**: Good at her trade, but not invincible (makes mistakes)

**PRIMARY PILLAR**: Relatability (reader connects through emotional journey)
**GROWTH PILLAR**: Proactivity (learns to act instead of avoid)
**CONSISTENT PILLAR**: Capability (maintains expertise throughout)

### Character Architecture

**MOTIVATION**:
- **Surface Want**: Find out who she was
- **Deep Need**: Accept painful truth and move forward
- **Conflict**: Truth = danger, but ignorance = living a lie

**PERSONALITY**:
- **Approach**: Initially cautious, becomes bold
- **Treatment of Others**: Protective (overcompensates for her own losses)
- **Style**: Meticulous about others' memories, careless with her own

**VALUES**:
1. Autonomy (hates being controlled)
2. Truth (even when painful)
3. Protection of innocent
- **Value Conflict**: Her protection instinct conflicts with truth-seeking when truth endangers people

### Voice Development

**Speech Patterns**:
- Uses memory/storage metaphors naturally ("That idea's corrupt, needs defragging")
- Self-interrupts when touching on painful topics
- Direct with clients, hesitant about herself

**Example Dialogue**:
> "The memory's clean. Third-party verified, no degradation, full sensory. You'll remember the taste of the coffee, the exact shade of her eyes when she said yes. Price is—wait, you sure you want this? Marriage proposals don't always… never mind. Not my business. Five hundred."

**Quirk**: Prices memories by emotional weight, not market value (reveals her values)

### Character Behavior Consistency

**Baseline**:
- **In danger**: Freezes first, then acts (trauma response)
- **When lying**: Over-explains (tells truth in profession, struggles with lies)
- **With authority**: Defensive, assumes corruption
- **Under stress**: Organizes physical objects (coping mechanism)

**Out of Character Moments Must Be Justified**:
- If she suddenly trusts authority → Show why (person reminds her of someone)
- If she forgets professional caution → Explain (emotional override)

### ✅ Character Skill Integration: WORKS

All three pillars create complex, believable protagonist who drives story emotionally.

## SKILL 3: SANDERSON'S LAWS OF MAGIC

### Magic System: Memory Trading

**EMOTIONAL CORE**:
- **Feeling**: Unease, loss, violation, nostalgia
- **Role**: Central to plot (mystery is IN memories)
- **Themes**: Identity, choice, trauma, truth

### LAW ONE: Understanding Equals Magic

**System Type**: **HARD** (Sera will solve climax with memory mechanics)

**Reader Must Understand**:
1. Memories can be extracted as physical "chips"
2. Extraction causes loss in original person
3. Chips can be viewed, sold, destroyed
4. Viewing someone else's memory = experiencing as if you lived it
5. Damaged chips = fragmented/corrupted memories
6. Multiple extractions from same event = different perspectives
7. Cannot extract what someone doesn't consciously remember (unconscious memories hidden)

**Established Early** (Ch 1-3):
- Show full extraction process
- Demonstrate viewing experience
- Explain limitations through failed extraction
- Cost visible (character loses memory, looks confused)

**Climax Use**:
Sera uses established rules: Triggers her father's oldest memory of the murder (unconscious guilt), which forces conscious remembering, which she then extracts as evidence.

### LAW TWO: Limitations > Powers

**LIMITATIONS** (Can't Do):
- Cannot create memories (only extract existing)
- Cannot modify memories (only view as-is)
- Cannot extract unconscious memories directly
- Cannot force someone to view a memory
- Range: Must have physical contact for extraction

**WEAKNESSES**:
- Chips are fragile (can be destroyed)
- Strong emotion during extraction corrupts chip
- Repeated viewing degrades memory
- Some people immune (rare genetic trait)

**COSTS**:
- **Physical**: Headaches, nosebleeds from frequent extraction
- **Mental**: Extractors slowly lose ability to distinguish own memories from viewed ones
- **Social**: Memory trading is legal but morally gray
- **Personal**: Each extraction risks losing something forever

**STRATEGIC VALUE**:
- Fragility means evidence can be destroyed (creates tension)
- Corruption from emotion explains why murder memory is fragmented
- Loss distinguishing memories explains Sera's confusion about her past
- Illegal black market creates stakes (Sera risks arrest)

### LAW THREE: Expand, Don't Add

**SINGLE CORE MECHANIC**: Physical memory extraction and viewing

**EXPANSION (Going Deep)**:

**Creative Applications**:
- **Basic**: Extract memory, sell it
- **Intermediate**: Multiple perspective reconstruction (piece together event from 3 people's memories)
- **Advanced**: Memory forensics (analyze corruption patterns to detect lies)
- **Master**: Trigger unconscious recall through sensory prompts, then extract

**Societal Impact**:
- **Legal**: Courts accept memory evidence (but debated)
- **Economic**: Memory tourism industry (experience others' lives)
- **Therapy**: PTSD treatment via extraction
- **Crime**: Memory theft black market
- **Class**: Rich preserve good memories, poor sell them for cash
- **Culture**: "Authentic experience" vs "Living through screens" debate

**Thematic Connection**:
- Theme: "Who are you without your memories?"
- Magic embodies theme (literally trade identity)
- Character arc reflects theme (Sera reclaiming her identity)

**BEFORE ADDING SECOND MAGIC**:
- ✅ Explored extraction forensics
- ✅ Considered economic system
- ✅ Integrated thematically
- ✅ Multiple complexity levels
- **Decision**: ONE system sufficient; depth > breadth

### Magic Consistency Check

**Every Use Follows Rules**:
```
CH 1: Sera extracts client's birthday memory
- ✓ Physical contact made
- ✓ Cost paid (client forgets birthday)
- ✓ Chip created (physical object)
- ✓ Limitation respected (client willing)

CH 6: Sera tries to extract killer's memory
- ✓ Physical contact attempted (grabs killer's arm)
- ✓ Weakness shown (killer has immunity - rare trait)
- ✓ Limitation kicks in (cannot extract unconscious memory directly)
- ✓ Failure has consequences (Sera's attempt alerts killer)
```

**No Rule Violations**: Every magical solution uses established mechanics.

### ✅ Magic Skill Integration: WORKS

System follows all three laws, creates both plot possibilities and meaningful limitations.

## SKILL 4: WORLDBUILDING TOOLS

### THREE COMPONENTS

**1. PHYSICAL SETTING**: Near-future urban city (2045)

**2. CULTURAL SETTING**: Memory-trading economy

**3. MAGIC SYSTEM**: Memory extraction (detailed above)

### CORE PRINCIPLES

**ICEBERG TECHNIQUE**:

**Visible (10%)**:
- Memory clinics on every corner
- People wearing "Memory Verified" badges
- News mentions "authenticity crisis"
- Sera's daily work as broker

**Hidden (90%)**:
- Full history of memory technology discovery
- Government regulation debates
- Other cities' different approaches
- International memory trafficking networks

**Purpose**: World feels deep without exposition dumps

**PYRAMID OF ABSTRACTION**:

**Abstract Concept**: "Memory trading changes society"

**Sensory Grounding**:
- **Sight**: Neon signs advertising "Premium Experiences - Only $99"
- **Sound**: Subtle static when viewing memories (tech artifact)
- **Smell**: Antiseptic scent of extraction clinics
- **Touch**: Warm chip in palm, slight vibration
- **Taste**: Metallic taste lingers after viewing violent memory
- **Emotion**: Disorientation when memory ends, snapping back to self

**Example Passage**:
> "The memory chip warmed her palm, a gentle pulse like a heartbeat. She slid it into the viewer. Static crackled—that familiar electric anticipation—and then she WAS the memory. Coffee scalded her tongue (not hers, his), morning light (too bright, he needed glasses), and her own face (younger, smiling, accepting his proposal)."

### CULTURAL WORLDBUILDING

**RELIGION/BELIEFS**:
- **Emerging Belief**: "Memory Purists" - believe traded memories corrupt soul
- **Practice**: Refuse any memory viewing, wear distinctive pins
- **Conflict**: Tension with mainstream acceptance
- **Story Impact**: Sera's partner is Purist (relationship strain)

**GOVERNMENT**:
- **Regulation**: Memory Trading Commission (like FDA)
- **Legal Status**: Legal with license
- **Black Market**: Unlicensed trading, stolen memories
- **Enforcement**: Memory Crimes Unit

**ECONOMICS**:
- **Currency**: Still uses dollars, but memory value fluctuates
- **Class Division**:
  - **Rich**: Hoard best memories, buy experiences
  - **Middle**: Selective trading
  - **Poor**: Sell memories to survive (memory poverty)
- **Jobs**: Memory brokers, authenticators, therapists, thieves

**SOCIAL CUSTOMS**:
- **Greeting**: "Remember me?" (ironic, since memories unreliable)
- **Dating**: Memory-sharing as intimacy test
- **Privacy**: Keeping memories is seen as secretive/suspicious
- **Taboo**: Viewing dead person's memories without permission

### AVOIDING PITFALLS

**✅ No Info Dumps**:
- World revealed through Sera's work (shows, doesn't tell)
- Client interactions expose economic system
- Conflicts demonstrate social tensions

**✅ Consistent Technology**:
- Memory tech established early
- Used consistently throughout
- Doesn't suddenly expand capabilities

**✅ Not Generic**:
- Specific memory-trading culture (not just "cyberpunk city")
- Unique social dynamics
- Fresh take on identity/technology theme

**✅ Story-Service Priority**:
- Every detail affects Sera's journey
- Memory tech creates her problem AND her solution
- Culture creates obstacles (Memory Purist partner, Commission regulations)

### CONSISTENCY TRACKING

**World Bible Entry**:
```
LOCATION: New Meridian City
POPULATION: 2 million
TECH LEVEL: 2045 (memory tech + near-future)
MEMORY LAWS:
- Licensed trading: Legal
- Unlicensed trading: Misdemeanor
- Memory theft: Felony
- Court evidence: Admissible with authentication
ECONOMY: Market-based, memory value fluctuates
```

### ✅ Worldbuilding Skill Integration: WORKS

All three components create cohesive, immersive world that serves story.

## CROSS-SKILL INTEGRATION TEST

### How Skills Reinforce Each Other

**Plot → Character**:
- Major Dramatic Question (Will she recover past?) drives Sera's character arc
- Yes, But / No, And pattern forces her to become more proactive
- Try-Fail Cycle enables growth from passive to active

**Character → Plot**:
- Sera's relatability makes reader care about answers to dramatic question
- Her capability (memory expertise) enables plot investigation
- Her proactivity growth creates natural escalation

**Magic → Plot**:
- Hard magic system allows satisfying magical climax
- Limitations create obstacles (fragile chips, immunity, unconscious barriers)
- Costs make choices meaningful (what to extract vs. preserve)

**Magic → Character**:
- Memory loss reflects Sera's emotional avoidance
- Rules mirror her internal struggle (unconscious vs. conscious)
- System limitations force character growth (can't magic away problems)

**World → Plot**:
- Memory economy creates inciting incident (valuable murder memory appears)
- Legal system creates stakes (black market danger)
- Social tensions add complications (Purist partner disapproves)

**World → Character**:
- Memory culture shaped Sera's values and fears
- Economic pressure explains her profession
- Social customs create relationship dynamics

**World → Magic**:
- Society built around memory tech feels logical
- Economic system makes sense given technology
- Cultural debates emerge naturally from capabilities

### COMPLETE STORY FLOW USING ALL FOUR SKILLS

**ACT ONE** (Chapters 1-4):

**Promise** (Skill 1): Establish tone (dark psychological), Major Dramatic Question (will she recover past?), character wants (truth vs. safety)

**Character** (Skill 2): Introduce Sera showing all three pillars - Medium Proactive (takes jobs but avoids personal memories), High Relatable (Save the Cat with elderly client), Medium Capable (expert broker with limitations)

**Magic** (Skill 3): Demonstrate memory extraction rules through routine jobs (Law 1), show costs and limitations (Law 2), explore societal integration (Law 3)

**World** (Skill 4): Reveal memory-trading culture through Sera's daily work (Iceberg), ground abstract concept in sensory details (Pyramid), establish social tensions (Cultural)

**ACT TWO** (Chapters 5-10):

**Progress** (Skill 1): Use Yes,But/No,And pattern to escalate (finds clues BUT recognizes herself, tries to investigate NO and becomes target), implement Try-Fail Cycle (first two failed attempts)

**Character** (Skill 2): Grow proactivity (moves from passive selling to active investigating), maintain relatability (self-awareness about avoidance), test capability (skills help but don't solve everything)

**Magic** (Skill 3): Deepen exploration (memory forensics, perspective reconstruction), maintain consistency (every use follows established rules), raise stakes (chips can be destroyed)

**World** (Skill 4): Complicate with social/legal consequences (black market danger, Commission investigation, Purist partner conflict), maintain consistency (world rules enforced)

**ACT THREE** (Chapters 11-15):

**Payoff** (Skill 1): Answer Major Dramatic Question (she recovers past AND stops killer), deliver Gandalf Promise (callback to "brokers forget important sales"), provide surprising but inevitable resolution (father is killer, she witnessed it, she chose to forget)

**Character** (Skill 2): Complete arc (full proactivity - chooses to confront), satisfy growth (passive avoider → active truth-seeker), maintain capability balance (skills matter but don't make it easy)

**Magic** (Skill 3): Use established rules for climax (trigger unconscious memory, extract as evidence), respect limitations (must get physical contact, father nearly escapes), pay costs (Sera's headaches from overuse, risk of losing more memories)

**World** (Skill 4): Deliver consequences (Commission arrests father, Purist partner decides to stay, Sera's reputation improves), tie up cultural threads (Memory Purist movement gets validation), maintain consistency (world works same way at end as beginning)

**✅ Rules followed** - One potential hole caught and fixed

## PRACTICAL APPLICATION

### For Writers Using These Skills

**Planning Phase**:
1. Start with core concept
2. Apply **World** skill to build setting
3. Apply **Magic** skill to define systems
4. Apply **Character** skill to create cast
5. Apply **Plot** skill to structure narrative

**Drafting Phase**:
- **Plot** guides scene-to-scene progression
- **Character** ensures POV authenticity
- **Magic** maintains consistency
- **World** provides sensory grounding

**Revision Phase**:
- **Plot**: Check promises, progress, payoff
- **Character**: Audit pillars, voice, arc
- **Magic**: Verify rule consistency
- **World**: Ensure coherence
- **Plot-Hole Prevention**: Track knowledge, timeline, behavior

### Integration Workflow

**Instead of Applying Sequentially** (Plot → Character → Magic → World),
**Apply Simultaneously** with each informing the others:

- "My character's proactivity (Character) drives the Yes,But pattern (Plot) using memory extraction rules (Magic) within a society that regulates this (World)"

- "My plot payoff (Plot) requires character growth (Character) using established magic (Magic) that the world allows (World)"

All four skills working together create something greater than the sum of their parts.
