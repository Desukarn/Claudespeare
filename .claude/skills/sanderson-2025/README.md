## Core Skills (Start Here)

### 1. **Promise, Progress, Payoff** (`promise-progress-payoff.md`)
**Purpose**: Structure engaging plots that keep readers turning pages

**Key Concepts**:
- **Promise**: Establish reader expectations (tone, story, character, structure)
- **Major Dramatic Question**: Central question driving the entire narrative
- **Progress**: Create meaningful forward momentum (signposting techniques)
- **Yes, But / No, And**: Escalation pattern for every scene
- **Try-Fail Cycle**: Three-attempt structure that forces character growth
- **Payoff**: Deliver satisfying, surprising-yet-inevitable conclusions
- **Gandalf Technique**: Setup → Forgotten → Delivered at darkest moment

**Use When**: Planning plot structure, outlining, revising pacing issues

**Common Mistakes Prevented**:
- Wheel-spinning scenes (no progress)
- Unclear story questions
- Predictable payoffs
- Broken promises to readers

---

### 2. **Proactive, Relatable, Capable** (`proactive-relatable-capable.md`)
**Purpose**: Create compelling protagonists readers love to follow

**Key Concepts**:
- **The Character Triangle**: Balance three essential pillars
  - **Proactivity**: Character drives the plot, makes choices
  - **Relatability**: Reader connection through emotion, flaws, "Save the Cat"
  - **Capability**: Skills and competence (balanced with limitations)
- **The Sliding Scale**: Adjust each pillar based on story needs
- **Character Arcs**: Track growth across triangle positions
- **Ensemble Cast Management**: Differentiate multiple protagonists
- **POV Voice Consistency**: Maintain distinct character voices

**Use When**: Character creation, fixing passive protagonists, balancing Mary Sues

**Common Mistakes Prevented**:
- Passive protagonist syndrome
- Unlikeable characters without compensation
- Inconsistent character behavior
- Flat character arcs

---

### 3. **Sanderson's Laws of Magic** (`sanderson-laws-of-magic.md`)
**Purpose**: Design magic systems that enhance rather than undermine story

**Key Concepts**:
- **First Law**: Understanding → Ability to resolve conflict with magic
  - Hard Magic: Fully explained, can solve climax
  - Soft Magic: Mysterious, creates problems more than solves them
- **Second Law**: Limitations > Powers
  - Costs, weaknesses, and flaws create tension
- **Third Law**: Expand before adding
  - Deep exploration of one system > shallow exploration of many
- **The Hard-Soft Spectrum**: Choose appropriate level for your story
- **Execution Techniques**: How to write soft vs. hard magic scenes

**Use When**: Designing magic systems, fixing deus ex machina issues, worldbuilding

**Common Mistakes Prevented**:
- Inconsistent magic rules
- Deus ex machina resolutions
- Overpowered magic removing tension
- Unexplained magic solving climax

---

### 4. **Worldbuilding Tools** (`worldbuilding-tools.md`)
**Purpose**: Create immersive, coherent worlds without info-dumping

**Key Concepts**:
- **Coherence Over Density**: Consistency > amount of detail
- **The Iceberg Approach**: Know more than you show
- **Sensory Grounding**: Engage all five senses
- **Cultural Coherence**: How core changes affect society
- **The Prime Mover**: One change to our world → explore consequences
- **Avoiding Info Dumps**: Show through action, not exposition

**Use When**: Creating settings, fixing exposition problems, establishing world rules

**Common Mistakes Prevented**:
- Frontloading exposition
- Inconsistent world rules
- Flat, generic settings
- Info dump paragraphs

## Supplementary Skills

### 5. **Plot Hole Prevention & Consistency Tracking** (`plot-hole-prevention.md`)
**Purpose**: Catch common writer errors before they reach readers

**Addresses User-Identified Issue**: *"characters knowing and speaking things they shouldn't in that moment of the story"*

**Key Systems**:
- **Character Knowledge Tracking**: Who knows what, when, and how
- **Timeline Consistency**: Travel times, ages, seasonal progression
- **Magic System Consistency**: Rules followed every time, costs paid
- **Worldbuilding Consistency**: Geography, culture, technology coherence
- **Character Behavior Consistency**: Voice, baseline actions, growth tracking
- **Consequence Tracking**: Every event has logical follow-through

**Use When**: Revision, consistency checking, catching plot holes

**Includes**:
- Character Knowledge Matrix templates
- Master Timeline tracking sheets
- Magic Use logs
- World Bible structure
- Revision checklists (Timeline, Knowledge, Magic, World, Character, Logic passes)

## How to Use These Skills

### For Outliners (Architects)

**Planning Phase**:
1. Start with **Promise-Progress-Payoff**: Define Major Dramatic Question, map Try-Fail Cycles
2. Use **Proactive-Relatable-Capable**: Build character triangle, plan arc
3. Apply **Sanderson's Laws**: Design magic system with clear rules and limitations
4. Use **Worldbuilding Tools**: Create coherent setting with sensory details

**Writing Phase**:
- Reference skills for specific guidance (scene escalation, character voice, magic consistency)

**Revision Phase**:
- Use **Plot-Hole Prevention**: Run all consistency checks (knowledge, timeline, magic, world)

---

### For Discovery Writers (Pantsers)

**First Draft**:
- Write freely without constraint

**Revision Phase**:
1. **Promise-Progress-Payoff**: Identify promises you made, ensure payoff delivers
2. **Proactive-Relatable-Capable**: Check character triangle balance, ensure growth arc
3. **Plot-Hole Prevention**: Character knowledge audit, timeline check, magic consistency
4. Fix issues found

**Benefit**: Skills catch common mistakes without constraining initial creativity

---

### For Revision-Heavy Writers

**After Complete Draft**:
1. Run **Plot-Hole Prevention** comprehensive audit
2. Fix flagged issues (character knowledge errors, timeline problems, magic inconsistencies)
3. Check **Promise-Progress-Payoff** alignment (promises established → paid off?)
4. Verify **Proactive-Relatable-Capable** balance (protagonist compelling?)
5. Polish with **Worldbuilding Tools** (sensory grounding, coherence)

## Final Notes

**Sanderson's Philosophy on Frameworks**:
> "Plot frameworks are tools that provide consistency without constraining creativity. Don't become a cook rather than a chef—frameworks should enhance your work, not limit it."

These skills are designed to:
- ✅ Prevent common mistakes (plot holes, passive protagonists, deus ex machina)
- ✅ Provide proven structure (Promise-Progress-Payoff, Try-Fail Cycles)
- ✅ Enhance creativity (frameworks as scaffolding, not straitjacket)
- ✅ Save time (automated consistency checking)
- ✅ Improve quality (measurable increases in coherence and reader satisfaction)

**Use them as tools, not rules. Your story always comes first.**
